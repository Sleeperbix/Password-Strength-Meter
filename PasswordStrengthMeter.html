Password Tips

Try and use as many different character types as you can in your passwords, lowercase, uppercase, numbers and special characters. When it comes to password cracking, your password is more secure the more different types of character are in your password.

Try creating passwords with at least 10 characters, but the longer the better. Short passwords can be cracked within seconds, while long passwords are more difficult and take much longer to crack.

Avoid using personal information in your passwords, such as your name, birthday, places you've lived or even your pet's name. Attackers can gain this information from a number of sources, including social media, and use it to guess your passwords.

Don't use passwords that are breached, even if they are rated as strong. Breached passwords are among the first passwords attackers will use to attempt to hack you.

Avoid using patterns or repeating characters in your passwords (e.g. qwerty, 123). Certain passwords such as 1qaz2wsx may look strong, but are among the most breached passwords of all time.

Frequently Asked Questions


How does this password strength meter work?▶

This tool evaluates your password based on length, character variety, entropy, and breach status.

What is password entropy?▶

Password entropy is a mathematical measure of password strength.
It calculates how many possible combinations exist based on your password's length and variety of characters.
For example, the password test has an entropy of 18.80, whereas t3$T has an entropy of 26.28.
While high entropy usually means a stronger password, it's not the only factor in a secure password.

Is it safe to enter my password here?▶

Yes. This tool does not store or send your password anywhere. However, a hashed portion of it is used to check if it has been breached.
You can learn more about this process here.

What is password hashing?▶

Password hashing is a one-way process that converts your password into a scrambled string of characters.
Websites usually store hashed passwords instead of plain text passwords. When you log in, your password is hashed again and compared to the stored hash. If they match, you gain access.
For example, the password !MaroonGravityPin32 might be hashed as:

What does it mean if my password is breached?▶

If your password is breached, it means it has been leaked online. This could be due to a data breach of a service you use, or falling victim to a phishing attack.
Most breached passwords are leaked in a hashed format, meaning attackers must crack them before using them.
If your password has been breached, change it immediately and never reuse it.

What is password cracking?▶

Password cracking is the process of converting a hashed password back into plain text.
Attackers do this by hashing multiple guesses and comparing them to the stolen hash. If they find a match, the password is cracked.
